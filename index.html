About the Lab

Bridging AI & Molecular Science

The Chen Lab at HKUST's Department of Chemical & Biological Engineering focuses on understanding complex soft matter systems through the lens of computational science and artificial intelligence. We develop and apply multiscale molecular simulations alongside machine learning methods to unravel the physics of polymers, polyelectrolytes, and biomolecular condensates.

Our work spans from fundamental thermodynamics of phase separation to AI-driven protein design and drug delivery — pushing the frontier of AI for Science (AI4S).

What We Do

Research Areas

We combine physics-based molecular simulations with modern machine learning to tackle fundamental and applied problems in soft matter and biomolecular science.

Polyelectrolyte Complex Coacervates

Investigating the structure, thermodynamics, and dynamics of polyelectrolyte complex coacervates — liquid-liquid phase-separated systems with broad applications in biomaterials, drug delivery, and underwater adhesives. We use coarse-grained and atomistic MD simulations to reveal molecular-level mechanisms.

Phase Separation Thermodynamics Coarse-Grained MD

Biocondensates & Biomolecular Assemblies

Studying the aging, rheology, and responsiveness of biomolecular condensates — membraneless organelles formed by liquid-liquid phase separation in cells. We probe how sequence, charge, and environmental conditions govern condensate material properties and function.

Biocondensates Rheology Aging

Charging Dynamics & Ion Transport

Exploring charging dynamics in electrolyte and polyelectrolyte solutions, and interfacial behavior of polymer complexes. Understanding ion transport mechanisms in polyelectrolyte systems is critical for energy storage, membrane separation, and biological signaling.

Ion Transport Electrolytes Interfaces

AI for Science (AI4S)

Developing and applying machine learning methods for protein design, drug delivery optimization, and polymer property prediction. We build physics-informed ML models that bridge the gap between atomistic detail and macroscopic behavior, enabling rational design of functional materials.

Machine Learning Protein Design Drug Delivery
